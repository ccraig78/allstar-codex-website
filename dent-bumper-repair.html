Dent, ding, scratch, and scuff repair

A small-looking dent can have cracked paint, a sharp crease, or damage across more than one body line. All-Star reviews the panel condition and paint before recommending a repair approach. Door dings, parking-lot damage, scratches, scuffs, and accident-related dents are all quoted by the actual condition.

Front and rear bumper repair

Bumper damage can involve the painted cover, mounting points, trim, lights, and nearby panels. The estimate may include repair, paint, replacement preparation, or related body work depending on what the inspection shows.

Hail damage evaluation

Hail can leave multiple dents across the roof, hood, trunk, and side panels. Clear photos can begin the conversation, while an onsite review helps determine the number and type of damaged panels and whether paint is affected.

What to send

Good photos help the shop assess the next step.

1

Whole vehicle

Take a wider photo that shows where the damage is located on the vehicle.

2

Close-up angles

Photograph dents, paint damage, panel edges, and bumper fitment from several angles.

3

Vehicle details

Include the year, make, model, claim status, and whether the vehicle is drivable.

Repair questions

Common questions about smaller body damage.

Should a bumper be repaired or replaced?

That depends on the material, cracks, mounting damage, paint condition, parts availability, and related damage. The shop can recommend an approach after inspection.

Can scratches be polished out?

Some marks are shallow while others go through the finish or accompany a dent. Photos help, but an onsite look gives the clearest answer.

Can you help with a hail insurance claim?

Bring your claim number and any paperwork you have. All-Star can help document visible damage and discuss the repair estimate.
