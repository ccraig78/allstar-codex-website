Start with the information you already have.

You do not need to have every answer before contacting the shop. If a claim is open, bring the claim number, adjuster or insurer contact information, existing estimate, and damage photos. If you have not opened a claim, the shop can still review the visible damage and discuss a self-pay estimate.

All-Star helps organize visible-damage documentation and repair planning. The repair estimate may cover panels, paint, bumpers, frame or structural concerns, parts, and related body work depending on the accident.

Parts and repair choices

Parts selection can depend on the repair, availability, claim, and customer preference. All-Star can discuss OEM parts or insurance-certified aftermarket options based on the specific job. Ask questions before approving the work so the estimate and repair plan are clear.

Claim repair checklist

  • Vehicle year, make, model, and VIN if available
  • Claim number and insurer information
  • Existing estimate or claim paperwork
  • Wide and close-up damage photos
  • Whether the vehicle is drivable
  • Your best callback number

Insurance repair questions

Answers to common claim questions.

Do I need a claim number before contacting you?

No. You can begin with photos and vehicle details. If a claim is already open, include the number and paperwork to make the conversation easier.

Can I pay for the repair myself?

Yes. All-Star helps both insurance and self-pay customers. The shop also offers financing; ask about the available payment options.

Is there a repair warranty?

All-Star offers a standard one-year warranty on repairs, with extended warranties considered case by case. Ask how it applies to your specific estimate.
